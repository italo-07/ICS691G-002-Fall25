# Title: Google ordered to pay $425.7 million in damages for improperly tracking smartphone activity
**Source:** [CBS News](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/google-ordered-pay-425-million-privacy-tracking-case/)

> [!NOTE]
> *This news report was summarized by **Yick Ching (Jeff) Wong***!

A verdict was delivered on Wednesday, September 3<sup>rd</sup>, 2025, at the federal courthouse in San Francisco,
following an extensive trial that lasted over two weeks. 
The class-action lawsuit accused Google of snooping on users’ smartphones without permission, 
and it covered roughly 98 million devices operating in the United States between July 1<sup>st</sup>, 2016, and September 23<sup>rd</sup>, 2024.

While Google had denied the accusation of improperly tracking users' online activity, the jury has ordered Google to pay $425.7 million in damages.
Google spokesman Jose Castaneda stated
> "This decision misunderstands how our products work, and we will appeal it, 
our privacy tools give people control over their data, and when they turn off personalization, we honor that choice."

While this might not be the end of this story, one of the lawyers who brought this case against Google, John Yanchunis of law firm Morgan & Morgan said
>"We hope this result sends a message to the tech industry that Americans will not sit idly by as their information is collected and monetized against their will."
